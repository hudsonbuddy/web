Int. Lab Room-Night

A door easily opens to reveal a dark lab room. A PERSON, silhouetted, pauses in the doorway and walks straight towards a desk. The person flips through papers and then takes a stack of papers. The person leaves quickly and closes the door behind. Keys are heard and the door CLICKS as it locks.

INT. CLASSROOM-DAY

DAVID sits at a table, with empty seats around him. LACEY sits near him. They are two of twenty people in a chemistry classroom. The TEACHER is writing on the board. David is nodding off. Lacey is paying attention. It is 8:30AM, or some early time.

Teacher

And then, we see that the double bond from the Oxygen on the 3 prime side of the strand-

DAVID

(mumbling)

Too...boring..,

Muffled sound from the teacher speaking as David is dozing off.

TEACHER

And that, is your homework for next time. Yes, I know, I just assigned homework on the first day. I have office hours sporadically, so just email me. Oh, and pick lab partners for lab next week. Lab is mandatory each week!

The teacher starts packing up and the class files out. David is fast asleep. The other students are pointing at him and  snickering.

TEACHER

(curtly)

Ignore him.

The teacher walks out. Lacey packs up her stuff, walks over to David and raises her hand slowly, with the intention of waking him up, but stops. She smiles and walks out.

Campus Security Officers DAVID and TOBEY are sitting at a table, watching television. David's head is in his hands.

Tobey

Mind like parachute, only works when open.

David

Charlie Chan.

TOBEY

The stuff that dreams are made of.

DAVID

Sam Spade, from The Maltese Falcon.

I'll make him an offer than he can't refuse.

OK then, how about: Excellent, my dear Watson.

DAVID

First of all, it's 'elementary' and second-

Chief

(over the radio)

Tobey? You there?

When you remove the impossible, whatever remains, however improbable, must be the truth.

CHIEF

Stop it Tobey, we just got a call.

Excellent, my dear Watson.

CHIEF

You realize its elementary, not excellent, and also-

DAVID

What have you got for us sir?

There's been a break-in over at the chemistry building. Tobey, will you show David here the ropes?

TOBEY

Alrighty, then.

David sits and blinks. Tobey is smiling and waiting for a response. STATIC breaks the silence.

CHIEF

Just get over there Tobey.

TOBEY

It's from Ace Ven-

Just go.

INT. LAB ROOM-DAY

DAVID and TOBEY walk towards the lab room, LACEY is standing outside with her lab partner, CAMI. Cami is comforting Lacey, who looks like had cried a while ago. A BRO sits at a chair near the lab, looking at his computer, and listening to music. A JANITOR is seen outside the lab a few doors down.

TOBEY

(to David)

Lemme show you how its done.

DAVID

(smiling)

Don't let me stop you then, heh.

David and Tobey walk to Lacey and Cami. They enter the lab room. The room is clean except that Lacey's desk is a mess and the lock to the door is broken off.

TOBEY

It's going to be alright, we're Campus Security and-

DAVID

(already looking around)

What happened here?

CAMI

We came in today to the lab like normal, when we saw that the lock had been mauled, and the desk was a mess. We started cleaning up when we realized that-

LACEY

(still a little distraught)

It's gone! My last year...

CAMI

Lacey has been working on her thesis paper on pharmaceuticals and it was gone. We checked everywhere for it, but its all gone.

Tobey nods and is listening, but David is already flipping through the papers and looking at the lock. GARY, a pre-med student who also works in the lab, walks in confidently.

Gary

(chuckling)

What's going on here, a circus?

TOBEY

(suspiciously)

And who are you?

LACEY

That's Gary, he works in our lab too. He's a douchebag.

GARY

(still cocky)

No love today either huh? It's almost halfway to my half birthday! Whatever.

Gary sits down at his desk and starts up the computer.

TOBEY

Do you know anything about Lacey's project she's been working on?

GARY

Yeah, the one on compound purification. What happened, did it get stolen?

Tobey looks at David, who stops flipping through papers. They share a beat. Lacey starts getting angry and walks toward Gary.

LACEY

You stole my paper didn't you! You couldn't handle the stress that your project wasn't going to be ready for the defense and decided to sabotage mine!

GARY

(cocky)

Hey, why else would campus security be here? I'm innocent babe, can't you see it in my eyes?

LACEY

You're disgusting.

LACEY

(to Tobey)

I'm sure it was him.

TOBEY

Well, what's your alibi Gary?

GARY

I was getting a drink at the Bull City, ask anyone who was there.

CAMI

I was there, and I did not see you all night.

GARY

(looking back at his computer)

Whatever.

LACEY

He's guilty! Arrest him!

TOBEY

We can't do that without any actual proof, and there is, uh, no proof.

David is back to looking at the papers and fumbling around the lock.

DAVID

This is very curious. This lock looks like it was broken off very brutally. Gary had the key, so he wouldn't go through the trouble and making a scene and breaking this lock. That doesn't make sense.

David circles around to the desk. Everyone is listening intently. David has an effect on the room.

DAVID

Also, this desk, it's a complete mess. Look at Gary's desk, its very neat, his clothes are pressed, and he's an anal pre-med. It's not like him to fumble around like this.

GARY

Well there you have it Lacey, it wasn't me! Now can we finally kiss and make up?

The janitor outside starts emptying the trash in the room.

TOBEY

He didn't say it wasn't you, you could have done all this to cover up the fact that you in fact did the deed. Fact.

DAVID

(to the janitor)

Were you here last night?

Janitor

Yeah I was. What happened?

DAVID

Break-in. Can you tell us who was here last night?

JANITOR

(trying to remember)

Hm...well, I was here for the night shift, and I think I saw that boy over there for sure-

TOBEY

Ha!

JANITOR

And...I think I saw that young lady. (Pointing to Cami) And, that guy over there, he's been there all night. (Pointing to the bro, who was looking up and immediately looks down.)

DAVID

(pointing to the bro)

That guy over there? Come on Tobey, let's have a chat with him. He might have seen who broke in.

TOBEY

Excuse us ladies.

David and Tobey walk over to the bro. The bro is already starting to pack up. As David gets closer he sees a gold pin with an owl on his shirt.

DAVID

Hey, sorry to interrupt but-

BRO

(packing up)

Well you are. I have to go to class man.

DAVID

Like I said, we're sorry, but we just have to ask you a few questions, starting with what's your name? I can't interrogate a stranger. (extends hand)

Drake

(shakes hand)

Call me Drake. What do you guys want.

TOBEY

There was a break-in at the chem lab over there, the janitor said he saw you last night. Did you see anything that happened?

DRAKE

No, I was here until like 10, and then I went back to my room to nap and I got here this morning to cram again. Too suspicious for you?

TOBEY

Do you have a roommate that can confirm that story?

DRAKE

No, I live in a single. Now if you don't mind, I have to go.

Drake leaves quickly, David's gaze follows Drake as he exits. They return to the lab room.

TOBEY

(to Cami and Lacey)

We're going to look over our notes back at our place, call us if you two get any leads. Did you not save any drafts on the computer?

LACEY

I did, but its scattered drafts, I wrote all my notes down on paper...Take Gary in with you and question him, he's the only person mean enough to steal my work!

DAVID

We'll try our best.

David and Tobey exit.

DAVID

(to Tobey as they leave)

I think I'm on the right track, but we need proof, Tobey. Everything we have now, it's all useless without real, concrete proof.

BLACK

Int. university Boardroom-Day

ADMINISTRATOR BROADHEAD sit on a panel of another ADMINISTRATORS, including PROFESSOR SYLVESTER. DAVID sits in front of them, exhausted.

BROADHEAD

We will look into these claims, but the thing is, that's all they are, claims. You have no proof.

DAVID

I'm telling you guys, I'm not just making this up! There's a secret society on campus! It's called-

BROADHEAD

I'm sure you have a well-prepared backstory to all this but frankly-

DAVID

Order of the Owl. How about that?

A beat as Broadhead is taken aback.

DAVID

They're the ones who are behind it all! They have people all over campus and-

BROADHEAD

No David, you were the one behind it all. You were the person who supplied cocaine and other illegal substances to over a third of our student body. We know this because we have proof. Understand?

DAVID

Why won't you listen?! I'm telling you-

PROFESSOR SLY

I'm sorry David, I really am. But this has to stop. Such a shame. You were such a bright student. Such promise when you first walked into these halls.

BROADHEAD

But you need to take responsibility for your actions. We are not doing this for us, we are doing this for you, and that's the truth. I admire you, David, maybe our paths will cross again someday.

Silence as David is led out in handcuffs. Broadhead's gaze follows him out with curiosity.

BLACK

Int. Bull City Bar-Night

SHEEN, a Durham police officer swings open the door and goes to the bar. DAVID and TOBEY are sitting in a booth, sharing a drink.

Sheen

(to bartender)

I'll have a Manhattan, Walker Blue, up and as dry as you can make it.

David sees Sheen and sits lower in his chair. Tobey keeps talking and is oblivious.

SHEEN

(to David)

David? Oh my god, David Harlan? Davey the Dealer? Haha are you serious?

DAVID

(curt)

Sheen.

TOBEY

You two know each other?

SHEEN

I went to college with this kid! This little prick got in when he was just 15! Haha this little prick thought he was the hottest thing since Megan Fox. How'd they ever let you back on campus?

TOBEY

(proud)

He's working with Campus Security. With me.

DAVID

Thanks, Tobey.

SHEEN

Davey the Dealer, working with campus security. Well I'll be. You see this? (holds up badge)

DAVID

You made detective?

Sheen

Youngest person in the department to make it. What's that? Hotshot? Me? Just trying to make a difference in the world baby.

DAVID

I'm happy for you Sheen. I really am.

TOBEY

(to Sheen, admiring)

Hey, you know what, you could help us with that case over at the chem building! There was this messy break-in and-

SHEEN

Shut up Tobey, you think there's something that you know that I didn't know? I already looked into it, and I didn't give a fuck. But now that I see that you two screw-ups are on it, I think I'll take this one from here.

DAVID

We don't need your help Sheen.

SHEEN

(as leaves)

I know you don't, but maybe that girl who got her shit jacked does, if you know what I mean...Anyway, deuces, I'm out.

Sheen exits.

DAVID

God damnit. I hate him.

TOBEY

Really? I think he's so cool. He has a gun too, making detective...

DAVID

Just be quiet. I'm leaving.

David gets up and leaves suddenly. Tobey calls a waiter for the check.

BLACK

Ext. Dreiser Campus Walkway-Night

DAVID strolls around as college students pass him in happy conversation. PROFESSOR SLY leaves a building and waves to David.

PROFESSOR SLY

Dave!

DAVID

(can't see who it is, muttering)

What the-

Professor Sly hurries toward David, who is waiting underneath a campus security light.

PROFESSOR SLY

(teasing)

Glad to see you're roaming about, keeping this place safe.

DAVID

Thanks prof, I'm trying.

PROFESSOR SLY

It's good to have you back at this place again, David. Dreiser wasn't the same without you.

DAVID

You have no idea how much I missed this place. I wanted a chance at growing up normally. I can't thank you enough for giving me a second chance.

PROFESSOR SLY

Hey, don't thank me, it was Professor Broadhead who first brought it up. As long as you behave for this community service security job, we should have you back on scholarship again.

David

Thanks, I had been in a bad mood before this.

PROFESSOR SLY

You were my best student, even at 15. Cocky yes, but confident. And an intuition like I've never seen! Physics, math, chemistry, actually, you were pretty bad at chemistry, I recall...

DAVID

(sheepishly)

Don't mention it. Just never really got it.

David and Professor Sly approach a parked car. Professor Sly gets in and rolls down window

PROFESSOR SLY

Well, this is me. Update me on how your job is going alright?

DAVID

I'm working on something now actually, not really going anywhere.

PROFESSOR SLY

I'm jealous of ya, you know. Not everybody gets a second chance. Make the most of yours.

DAVID

Don't be jealous, be happy.

Professor Sly rolls up the window, waves and drives off.

DAVID

(to himself, whispering slowly)

Don't be jealous, be happy.

David walks off and goes into a dorm.

Int. David's dorm Room-Night

ROBBIE, David's roommate, is playing video games. DAVID enters slowly.

Robbie

(not looking up)

My man, long shift?

DAVID

(weary)

Good night, Robbie.

ROBBIE

Man, you missed this great party tonight, tons of liquor gettin' thrown back, tons of bitches layin' out and everything.

DAVID

(still tired)

And you are here playing video games why?

David plops onto his bed.

ROBBIE

Newbs need pwning man. There's no such thing as newbs unless you have pros like me. Don't be jealous babe.

DAVID

(not tired this time)

Good night, Robbie.

David exits and Robbie keeps looking at the television.

INT. LAB ROOM-NIGHT

DAVID walks by DRAKE, who is sitting at the same place as earlier today. David walks into the dark lab room and fumbles around, similar to the first scene.

BLACK

Int. David's Dorm room-Day

DAVID sits at his desk, flipping through a directory. He takes and phone and makes a call.

DAVID

(on the phone)

Lacey? It's David from campus security. (pause) No, it's David, like the... thing, you know? (pause) Yeah, sorry I realized it made no sense as soon as I said it. Anyway, could you meet me at the chem lab today at 7? Cami and Gary will be there too, already called them. (pause) I'll see you there. (putting down the phone) Or...you could hang up on me, that's ok too.

INT. LAB ROOM-NIGHT

CAMI and LACEY are sitting down. GARY is standing near his desk, defensively. DAVID and TOBEY walk towards the room, and see DRAKE studying.

DAVID

(to Drake)

Hey, Drake, could you come over here? You're included in this investigation too man.

DRAKE

Uh, I just remembered I have class breh.

DAVID

(as he takes Drake's arm)

It'll only be a moment.

The three of them enter the lab room and all of them sit down except for David. Sheen enters.

DAVID

So guys, I brought you all here to-

SHEEN

Playing detective? Why not let an actual one do its job?

DAVID

Sheen. Well, what are you waiting for, you already interrupted me, go ahead.

SHEEN

Gladly. So I was looking through the police notes for this case, and I happened to run across a few suspicious occurrences. First, the door. It was obviously locked and then broken into, and quite violently too, I might add. The three people who work in this lab, Lacey, Cami and Gary, all have keys and thus would not bring attention to themselves by breaking this lock in the dead of night.

LACEY

I'm telling you, it's just a cover, Gary took it!

TOBEY

Yeah, I agree. You have no alibi and you have a clear motive. Let's go Gary, we're bringing you in.

SHEEN

Incorrect. We would be arresting the wrong person if we continue. The second suspicious occurance is the janitor's story about our friend Drake here. Always hanging out, day and night outside of this room? They say a culprit always returns to the scene of the crime, and here have it folks. The person who stole the fair lady Lacey's award winning paper is none other than our slippery friend Drake here!

Sheen turns around and points at Drake, who is shocked. Everyone's attention is on Drake except for David, who is looking down, deep in thought.

GARY

Ha. See?

SHEEN

That's right. Only a person without the key, and without knowledge of where the paper would be could cause that mess on the door and that mess on the desk. If we are careful, I know we can find fingerprints somewhere, just admit to the crime now Drake, I'll see to it in court that you don't see too much jailtime.

DRAKE

It wasn't me! I'm not going in without any concrete proof!

TOBEY

Save it, buster. We're taking you in now. Sorry, Gary.

DAVID

He's right Sheen, you have no proof, this is just speculation.

Sheen

I will get proof then, but Drake is most certainly, most definetly the culprit here.

DAVID

I think I would tend to disagree.

Everyone's attention is immeadieatly on David, who doesn't realize this. David is still looking down, in thought.

DAVID

Well, there's plenty of things that don't make sense in all this. (pause)

SHEEN

(annoyed)

Well, care to elaborate?

David

Fine. I was having such fun listening to your story too. I'll start from the top then. The first and most important piece of information is that of the janitor's.

LACEY

The janitor's?

DAVID

That's right. The janitor said that during his entire night shift, he only saw Drake, Gary and Cami here. That essentially puts an umbrella around these three, and only one of them could have stolen the paper. As to the times he saw them, he does not remember, he sees different people every night, I understand. Now if we look at each of these three, we see that our friend Gary here as the best motive and the worst alibi. Both Gary and Lacey have papers that might be in contention of undergraduate publication in Science and Nature magazine, a worthy prize for any up and coming researcher. Since Dreiser can only sponsor one article, removing the other paper from contention is the most surefire way.

TOBEY

Well, there you have it. Gary, let's do this.

GARY

Get your hands off me, I told you already I didn't take it.

DAVID

And I believe you. In my interaction with Gary, I see him as a competitive type, a hard worker and although not nice, an honest person. He is the type of person who would rather work harder on his own project than to sabotage another. He got here from nothing through hard work and he's proud of that. The due date for his draft is coming up and I think he came to the lab to put some finishing touches before his meeting with his advisor today.

CAMI

Gary, why didn't you just say so?

GARY

God damnit. I didn't want Lacey to know that I work on my project twice as hard as she does, and it still pales in comparison. It just comes to you so easily, Lacey, I've always admired that.

LACEY

I didn't know, gosh I feel bad I made you feel that way.

DAVID

And that brings us to Drake. Oh Drake, what to do with you. Going on with my series of events that night. I believe that Gary came in to work, then left within the hour. That's when Drake made his move. The locks on these doors are not easily broken, it requires someone of above average strength and determination, perhaps a lacrosse player?

LACEY

So it was you! Why? Why me?

DAVID

Hold on Lacey, I haven't finished yet. I do think that Drake broke this lock, and fumbled around the desk and made it a mess. I agree with Sheen on this account.

SHEEN

Humph.

DAVID

But the reason why it was a mess, was because the paper was already gone.

A silence in the room as everyone waits for David to continue.

DAVID

Which, in turn brings us to our last suspect, Cami.

LACEY

(to Cami)

Cami? Why?

Cami is at a loss for words, and David sees this awkwardness.

DAVID

I believe that Cami, having been your lab partner for these past two years, always being overlooked and never really given a chance, has been jealous of your talents, and stole your paper, on the eve of publication. And, when I was in here last night, I happened to go through some of Cami's drawers to find the complete works of Lacey Sherman in the bottom drawer.

CAMI

(starting to cry)

You've always been just, better than me! Whenever there's a question in class, you're always the one to answer, whenever we go out, it's you who always gets the most attention, even when we're just eating lunch, you always do the talking and I just listen! It's not fair! I try and I work but it just doesn't ever go right for me! I guess some people were just made for better things.

TOBEY

Alright, alright, this time we got it. Come with me.

Tobey starts to lead Cami out the door.

LACEY

That's not true.

Lacey pulls out her necklace from her drawer. It says Friends on it.

LACEY

Roommates and best friends forever right?

CAMI

(defeated)

Right.

Tobey and Cami leave, as well as Gary, who comforts Lacey.

SHEEN

(as he leaves)

Lucky guess, Davey. Drake, come by the station to fill out a damages form on the lock, and you'll be all set.

DAVID

Whatever man. (to Lacey) You know, I'm going to be taking the same chem class this semester, and since you don't have a lab partner...I was thinking-

LACEY

(curtly)

Just don't be lazy and don't be stupid.

GARY

And no flirting.

DAVID

Ok Gary, wishful thinking.

GARY

Well, I gotta get outta here, smell y'all later!

Drake, who has been quiet this entire time, follows after Gary.

DAVID

(seeing Drake leave)

I'll see you guys around, gotta run!

David follows Drake, who does not know David is following.

EXT. DREISER CAMPUS WALKWAY-NIGHT

Drake is walking quickly and with a purpose. David follows some yards behind.

DAVID

(to himself or VO)

What people didn't really pick up on is that Drake was trying to steal the paper too! Just because he didn't get to the paper doesn't mean anything! Now why would some jock lacrosse player want to know about synthetic compound purification anyway? Cocaine that's why!

Drake turns a corner, with David behind. As David looks around the corner to see Drake turning another corner, a hand is placed on his shoulder.

A wonderful night, isn't it?

Dr. Broadhead, you suprised me.

It's what I always do, isn't it?

I guess you're right. I haven't had a chance to thank you for taking me in again. I won't screw it up this time.

Don't worry, I trust you, I always have. You were just too young and foolish.

That's true, heh. A 15-year-old getting caught up in drugs.

I'll get over it. I just want you to succeed. It's a shame seeing a talent like yours go to waste. I'm assuming your at the top of your class once again?

Heh, classes haven't really kicked in yet, but I hope so. And chemistry this time could be a lot better, found a good lab partner.

Good to hear it. I'm always happy when your around you know that? You are truly a breath of fresh air on this campus.

DAVID

I try. And I too missed having someone to look up to.

BROADHEAD

So how is the job I found treating you? Any mysteries yet?

DAVID

(playfully)

Just one so far.
