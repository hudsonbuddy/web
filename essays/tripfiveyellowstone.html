At Yellowstone, the first night we camped in sub-zero weather and I felt like I was going to freeze. As soon as I got up, I went to the bathroom to heat up from the wind and to put my hands on the dryer. Showers and hot water there cost $10. We ate breakfast at their lodge and it was really warm. The food and environment was exactly a ski lodge. The workers were similar there too. We then drove to a different campsite and hiked Purple Mountain while tripping on the last of our acid.


After coming down from Purple Mountain we touched down in civilization, known as Salt Lake City to see their famous dried up lake and all their birds and poop. People were taking wedding photos in the wind and it was beautiful to see. We continued on, moving from nature to man with a stop in Las Vegas. I had never been there before, and I never knew how fake everything was, although in a good way. Paris, Venice, NYC, ancient Rome and Egypt all make appearances, and even the sky is painted blue. It was very walkable and we spent most of our money at cheap places, especially this one place called Bill’s, which also is coincidentally closed now. We played poker there and they had $5 blackjack and we just sat there and drank their free drinks. We wandered up and down the strip, doing the last of all of our drugs before going into California, as the trip was winding down. We ate the rest of our already-bad mushrooms, ecstasy and drank until we couldn’t stand up straight. The day we left Nick overdrew his bank account and gambled all of his money while Joo and I were sleeping and he comes back with double his money on a single hand of blackjack.


We spent the last part of the road trip in the Grand Canyon, watching a sunrise and doing a hike, but it wasn’t that fun. We drove through to SF and I met up with David, and started working, concluding the road trip. We drove almost 6000 miles, taking a month, and however romantic it may seem, it actually was.
